Exponential Growth of Technology Capabilities by Raymond Kurzweil

In his 2006 book The Singularity Is Near, Ray Kurzweil explains the Law of accelerating returns, which predicts an exponential increase in technologies like computers, genetics, nanotechnology, robotics and artificial intelligence.

According to Kurzweil, we are now at the tipping point that will lead to an exponential growth in technology in the next few years.

Within the next five years drones will deliver food, packages and other goods directly to our homes

Regulations for commercial drones deliveries are planned to be created soon in USA, Europe and China. This will allow companies like Amazon, 7-Eleven and Domino's to deliver their products directly to our homes.

Shipments of consumer drones will more than quadruple over the next five years, fueled by increasing price competition and new technologies that make flying drones easier for beginners.

SMART TV AND STREAMING VIDEO IN 4K RESOLUTION BECOME COMMON IN DEVELOPED COUNTRIES

Traditional TV will be replaced by Smart TVs connected to the Internet. Users will be more and more encouraged to choose programs according to their personal tastes and at their own schedule.

As more content becomes available in a 4K resolution, increasing in internet bandwidth all around the world, thanks to fibre-optic network, will allow to stream it.

according to many experts Fully autonomous self-driving cars will debut in 2019

We divide the self-driving car into two different types: semi-autonomous and fully autonomous. A fully autonomous vehicle can drive from point A to point B and encounter the entire range of on-road scenarios without needing any interaction from the driver. These will debut in 2019.

Large-scale automakers like Ford, BMW and GM predicted, though, 2021 would be a more realistic date to expect self-driving cars on the roads.

internet of things is poised to explode by 2020

In the next years major competitors will continue to improve the technology and the standardisation required for IoT. As a result, by the end of this decade the world will be a more deeply connected place.

The huge amount of data resulting from a wide network of smart objects connected to each other will lead to significant improvements. By 2020 IoT could cross the turning point towards a rapid spread.

In the early 20's 3D printing will begin to spread in developed countries

As a result of exponential improvements that will lead to more faster and less expensive machines, in the early 20s, 3D printing could emerge as a mainstream consumer technology.

Thanks to 3D printing, in the future we'll be able to produce some of our consumer goods, like custom clothes and spare parts, reducing at near-zero the marginal cost of the process.
